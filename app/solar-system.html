Mercury

The smallest planet and closest to the Sun — a cratered world of extreme temperatures.

57.9M km from Sun
Fact: A single day on Mercury lasts 176 Earth days.

Venus

Earth's toxic twin — wrapped in thick CO₂ clouds with crushing, furnace-like heat.

108.2M km from Sun
Fact: Venus spins backwards, and its day is longer than its year.

Earth

The only known world with life — blue oceans, breathable air and a protective magnetic field.

149.6M km from Sun
Fact: Earth is the densest planet in the solar system.

Mars

The Red Planet — home to the tallest volcano and the deepest canyon in the solar system.

227.9M km from Sun
Fact: Olympus Mons on Mars is nearly 3× taller than Everest.

Jupiter

The giant of the solar system — more massive than all other planets combined.

778.5M km from Sun
Fact: Its Great Red Spot is a storm larger than Earth, raging for centuries.

Saturn

The jewel of the solar system — crowned by rings of ice and rock stretching 280,000 km.

1.43B km from Sun
Fact: Saturn is so light it would float in water.

Uranus

An ice giant tipped on its side, rolling around the Sun with seasons lasting 21 years.

2.87B km from Sun
Fact: Uranus rotates at a 98° tilt — it essentially orbits on its side.

Neptune

The windiest world — a deep-blue ice giant with supersonic storms of 2,100 km/h.

4.50B km from Sun
Fact: Neptune was found by mathematics before it was seen through a telescope.
