The Voyage Outward

Space Missions

From first footsteps on the Moon to telescopes that see the dawn of time — the missions that changed everything.

1969

Apollo Program

Apollo 11 carried Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin to the first human footsteps on the Moon, with Michael Collins orbiting above. Twelve astronauts walked the lunar surface across six landings.

NASA · Crewed Lunar Landings
1977

Voyager 1 & 2

The twin Voyagers toured the outer planets and kept going. Voyager 1 is now the most distant human-made object ever — over 24 billion km away, carrying the Golden Record into interstellar space.

NASA · Interstellar Probe
1990

Hubble Space Telescope

Orbiting above Earth's blurring atmosphere, Hubble revolutionised astronomy — measuring the universe's expansion, imaging galaxies in their infancy and delivering three decades of iconic deep-space views.

NASA / ESA · Space Observatory
1998

International Space Station

A football-field-sized laboratory orbiting every 90 minutes, the ISS has hosted continuous human presence in space since 2000 — the largest international engineering project ever attempted.

NASA · ESA · JAXA · CSA · Roscosmos
2008

Chandrayaan Program

ISRO's lunar program confirmed water molecules on the Moon (Chandrayaan-1), and in 2023 Chandrayaan-3 achieved a historic soft landing near the lunar south pole — a first for any nation.

ISRO · Lunar Exploration
2021

Mars Missions

NASA's Perseverance rover landed in Jezero Crater to hunt for signs of ancient microbial life and cache samples for return to Earth — while its Ingenuity helicopter made the first powered flight on another world.

NASA · Mars Exploration
2021

James Webb Space Telescope

Parked 1.5 million km from Earth, Webb's golden mirror sees the infrared universe — capturing the first galaxies after the Big Bang and analysing exoplanet atmospheres for signs of habitability.

NASA / ESA / CSA · Infrared Observatory
2022 →

Artemis Program

Artemis I flew an uncrewed Orion around the Moon, paving the way for astronauts to return to the lunar surface — including the first woman — and building toward humanity's next giant leap: Mars.

NASA & Partners · Return To The Moon
